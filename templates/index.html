Understanding the Weather Indicators

Our model uses 9 key meteorological and fire danger rating parameters to predict the Fire Weather Index

Temperature

Air Temperature in Celsius (°C)

The ambient air temperature at noon, measured in degrees Celsius. Higher temperatures increase fire risk by drying out vegetation and accelerating fire spread.

RH

Relative Humidity (%)

The percentage of moisture in the air relative to the maximum it can hold. Lower humidity means drier conditions and higher fire danger.

Ws

Wind Speed (km/h)

The velocity of wind measured in kilometers per hour. Wind accelerates fire spread and can carry embers to start new fires downwind.

Rain

Rainfall Amount (mm)

The total precipitation in millimeters over the previous 24 hours. Recent rainfall reduces fire risk by increasing moisture in fuels.

FFMC

Fine Fuel Moisture Code

A numeric rating of the moisture content of litter and fine fuels on the forest floor. Higher values indicate drier fine fuels and easier fire ignition (range: 0-101).

DMC

Duff Moisture Code

A rating of the average moisture content of loosely compacted organic layers of moderate depth. Indicates fuel consumption in moderate duff layers.

ISI

Initial Spread Index

A numeric rating of the expected rate of fire spread. It combines the effects of wind and FFMC on rate of spread without the influence of variable fuel amounts.

Classes

Fire / Not Fire Classification

Binary classification indicating whether a fire occurred (1) or not (0). This helps the model understand the relationship between weather conditions and fire occurrence.

Region

Geographical Region Code

The Algerian region where the observation was made. Region 0 represents Bejaia (northeast), and Region 1 represents Sidi Bel-abbes (northwest).
